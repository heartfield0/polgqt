About the Game

Tic-tac-toe, a game that has stood the test of time, has roots that trace back to the Roman Empire around the 1st century B.C. During this period, an early variation of the game was played, known as "terni lapilli," which translates to "three pebbles at a time." This ancient game was played using a grid, similar to the modern version of tic-tac-toe we know today. The remnants of this game have been discovered chalked all over Roman ruins, indicating its widespread popularity among the Romans. Interestingly, evidence of a similar game has also been unearthed in ancient Egyptian ruins, suggesting that this simple yet engaging game had found its place in different cultures and civilizations.
